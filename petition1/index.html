Why this petition matters?

Most of us might be already seeing erratic constructions of Cement roads all around the City and outskirts of our beloved Nagpur, they have not left out any lane or corner of the city without having a Cement road whether its a main road or a small Galli. While prima facie it looks to be a "Development" happening, but if if we deep dive into the basics of road infrastructures there are more disadvantages of Cement roads than that of Bitumen or Tar roads.

This year most of us have experienced many hotter evenings and warmer nights in summer season and the total number of warmer nights in Nagpur have touched a all time high this year, thanks to Cement roads combined with other factors responsible. Due to this the average temperatures have been rising above 46 - 47 degrees in summer whereas the surrounding regions outside of the core city had 2-3 degrees lesser.

Negative Impacts of Cement roads:

Urban Heat Island Effect: Concrete surfaces due to its light color reflect heat in surrounding air contributing to higher temperatures in urban areas in day and night compared to their rural counterparts.

Water Runoff and flooding: Concrete surfaces are impermeable, leading to increased stormwater runoff, which contribute to flooding and pollution of waterways with contaminants picked up from road surfaces.

Carbon Emissions: The production of cement, a key component of concrete, is a major source of CO2 emissions. The cement industry is responsible for approximately 8% of global CO2 emissions.

Resource Extraction: Concrete production requires significant amounts of natural resources, including sand, gravel, and limestone, leading to habitat destruction and biodiversity loss.

Energy Consumption: The manufacturing of cement is energy-intensive, requiring high temperatures that are typically achieved using fossil fuels.

Waste Generation: At the end of their life cycle, concrete roads generate a large amount of construction and demolition waste, which can be challenging to recycle.
